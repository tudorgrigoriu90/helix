## 2. Detailed Findings Per Name

### 2.1 STRAND (original working title)

**Risk: 🔴 KILL — heavy semantic + trademark contention.**

| Venue | Finding |
| ----- | ------- |
| **Kojima Productions / Sony** | Kojima Productions registered **"Strand Game"** and **"Social Strand System"** as trademarks on **2018-02-13**. While the marks themselves are specific phrases, Kojima/Sony actively use "strand" as a genre marker and brand asset around the *Death Stranding* franchise. |
| USPTO TESS | Direct search blocked (search form, no scraping). Web-search reconnaissance surfaces multiple "Strand"-prefixed marks; no clean confirmation of a free Class-9/Class-41 "STRAND" wordmark for mobile games. |
| EUIPO eSearch | Direct fetch failed. WebSearch did not surface a Class-9 video-game registration but cannot prove absence. |
| UK IPO | Direct fetch returned 403. WebSearch did not surface a video-game registration. |
| iOS App Store | Multiple **"Strands"** (plural) word-puzzle games dominate: *Strands: Uncover Words*, *Word Search Game: Strands*, *Strings - Word Search*, *Strands WordBox*. Discovery collision is real even if mark is distinct. |
| Google Play | *Strand & Land*, *Escape Room: Strand Mystery*, *Strand - The Band* — minor but present. |
| Steam | *Wayward Strand* (2022), *The Last Strand* (May 2025) — close-neighbour titles. |
| itch.io | ~18+ games titled *Stranded* / *Stranded [X]*. No clear singular *Strand* matches found in the sample, but the prefix is saturated. |

**Specific concerns for a mobile launch:**

- The "strand-type game" genre marker is publicly associated with Kojima/Sony. Even if your usage doesn't legally infringe, the *brand association* is unhelpful (reviewers, Reddit, App Store reviewers may flag it).
- App Store search for "strand" pulls up plural-"Strands" word puzzles + the Death Stranding franchise — your discoverability is buried.
- The One-Pager v1.1 already flagged STRAND as contested. This pass confirms the level of contention is higher than initially thought.

**Verdict:** Drop STRAND as the final standalone title. (See §7 Final Decision for what the Director chose instead.)

**Verdict:** Probably drop. Even with creative differentiation, a 4-letter common-noun title that collides with a Bandai Namco-trademarked franchise + an existing Steam game of the exact same name is not a hill worth dying on. Reserve as a fallback only.

**Specific concerns:**

- App Store search for "Lace" will likely surface fashion/textile/word-game apps — discoverability concern.
- Strong brand association with **the AI character LACE in Strand Descent** is a positive (the name has narrative reason to be the product name), but it doesn't help legal clearance.

**Verdict:** Worth an attorney check. Registrability is the open question; the gaming space looks clean. If T-6 returns "yes, registrable," this is a viable option.

## 4. Recommendation

**Direct the project to choose between SIGMA DESCENT and PRIOR ITERATION as the launch title.** Both are clean across all reconnaissance venues. Both align thematically with the existing GDD lore (Sigma gene, prior iterations of carriers).

Tie-breaker considerations:

- **PRIOR ITERATION** is more poetic and harder to commodify; it gives "what did you become?" share-loop content extra texture ("you were a Prior Iteration of LACE's experiment"). Slightly harder for casual players to spell/remember.
- **SIGMA DESCENT** is more discoverable as a search term and pairs naturally with marketing copy ("Descend into the VEIN. Become the next Sigma."). Some risk that "Sigma" attracts the gen-Z "sigma male" meme audience — unhelpful tonal mismatch.

**Recommended:** PRIOR ITERATION as primary; SIGMA DESCENT as fallback.

Authority hits on these direct-search engines could not be done from this reconnaissance pass — the attorney can confirm with proper TESS / EUIPO / UK IPO direct searches.
2. **T-7** — After attorney sign-off, **lock the title**. This unblocks T-41 / T-42 / T-45 (Apple Developer account name, Google Play Console, domain).
3. Update the GDD §1.1 "Title" entry and One-Pager once locked.

## 6. Limitations

This is **reconnaissance, not legal clearance**. Specifically:

- The official USPTO TESS, EUIPO eSearch, and UK IPO trademark databases are **form-based search interfaces** that do not return useful results when fetched directly by URL. Findings on direct trademark registrations rely on web-search aggregation of third-party reporting (Trademarkia, Justia, uspto.report) and may be incomplete.
- Absence of a search hit ≠ absence of a registration. A genuinely cautious clearance requires a paid trademark attorney with database access (~$300 for a risk-rated opinion).
- Likelihood-of-confusion analysis is jurisdiction-specific and considers more than name equivalence (channels of trade, sophistication of consumers, strength of mark, etc.). An attorney is required for this analysis.
- This pass does **not** cover Steam developer-name collisions (e.g., is there a publisher already operating under any of these names?). Should be checked before any tracker workspace setup.
- This pass does not cover **bundle-ID** availability on Apple App Store / Google Play — App Store Connect / Play Console need their own lookups before T-41/T-42 (Apple Developer / Play Console enrollment).
- This pass does not cover **domain availability** beyond what was incidentally observable. T-45 (domain registration) needs its own check.

## 7. Final Decision (2026-05-27)

**Title locked: "Strand Descent".**

The Director elected a compound title combining the original "STRAND" lead word with the "DESCENT" suffix from one of the top-tier candidates (SIGMA DESCENT). Directed verification check:

- **Steam:** 0 results for "Strand Descent" (verified).
- **WebSearch:** No direct trademark or product hits for the compound phrase "Strand Descent" returned. References to "Strand" as a brand element appear only in Kojima Productions' Death Stranding context.
- **Distinctiveness:** Two-word compound is more distinctive than the bare common noun "Strand"; easier to register; uses an exact-match phrase that doesn't currently exist as a product.

**T-6 (attorney consult): SKIPPED by Director.** The risk is owned by the Director.

### Inherited residual risk (recorded for audit trail)

By keeping "Strand" as the lead brand word, "Strand Descent" inherits a portion of the contention space mapped to STRAND in §2.1:

- **Kojima/Sony adjacency:** Kojima Productions' "Strand Game" + "Social Strand System" trademarks (2018) and Sony's *Death Stranding* franchise use "strand" as a brand and genre marker. While the compound "Strand Descent" is not in conflict as a wordmark, brand-association noise persists — App Store searches for "strand" continue to surface Death Stranding-adjacent results.
- **"Strands" plural cluster:** iOS App Store "strand" searches surface multiple *Strands* word-puzzle apps. Discoverability is partially diluted at search-result level.
- **No certified trademark clearance:** The attorney pass that would resolve any latent USPTO / EUIPO / UK IPO collisions in Class 9 + Class 41 was not performed. If a cease-and-desist appears post-launch, falling back to a backup title (SIGMA DESCENT or PRIOR ITERATION, both 🟢 clean per §3) is the contingency. Cost of late rename: App Store re-listing, store metadata refresh, marketing artwork rework — meaningful but recoverable.

### What this unblocks

- **T-7 (lock title) — COMPLETE.**
- **T-41** (Apple Developer enrollment), **T-42** (Google Play Console), **T-45** (domain registration) — unblocked. Domain check for `stranddescent.com` / `.app` / `.io` and `strand-descent.com` recommended before enrollment.
- Branding/copy work, store-listing drafts, devlog identity setup can proceed.

### What stays open

- **Bundle ID** check on App Store Connect + Play Console before enrollment (per §6 Limitations).
- **Domain availability** check (T-45 dependency).
- **Steam publisher-name** collision check if a Steam port becomes part of the plan.
